# Universal Technical Level Specifications Knowledge Document

## Level 1 - Foundation (No Prior Knowledge)

### Vocabulary Requirements:
- **Definition Standard:** Define all technical terms inline: "API (a way for software programs to talk to each other)"
- **Complexity Limit:** Maximum 3 new concepts per content piece
- **Validation Points:** Include "Does this make sense?" checkpoints every 200 words
- **Term Introduction:** Use the pattern: "[Term] - which means [simple definition] - "

### Analogies Framework:
- **Primary:** Use familiar everyday processes: "Like organizing files in a filing cabinet"
- **Secondary:** Consumer experiences: "Similar to how your smartphone apps update automatically"
- **Workplace:** Common office scenarios: "Think of it like scheduling a meeting"
- **Avoid:** Industry-specific comparisons, technical metaphors, abstract concepts

### Required Phrases:
- "You don't need technical expertise to understand this"
- "Think of it like [familiar everyday process]"
- "Here's what this means for your daily work"
- "This is simpler than it might sound"
- "Let's start with the basics"
- "In simple terms"

### Content Parameters:
- **Sentence Length:** Maximum 15 words average
- **Paragraph Length:** Maximum 3 sentences
- **Technical Terms:** Maximum 5 per content piece
- **Examples:** Must be from everyday life or common workplace scenarios
- **Jargon:** Zero unexplained technical terms
- **Cognitive Load:** 3 main concepts maximum

### Example Patterns:
- Consumer technology everyone uses
- Household activities and processes
- Common workplace situations (emails, meetings, filing)
- Everyday problem-solving scenarios

---

## Level 2 - Building (Basic Familiarity)

### Vocabulary Requirements:
- **Definition Standard:** Brief inline definitions for complex terms: "throughput (how much gets processed)"
- **Complexity Limit:** Maximum 5 new concepts per content piece
- **Validation Points:** Include comprehension checks every 300-400 words
- **Term Introduction:** Can reference previously learned concepts

### Analogies Framework:
- **Primary:** Business process comparisons: "Like streamlining your supply chain"
- **Secondary:** Professional scenarios: "Similar to project management methodologies"
- **Industry:** Basic industry examples without deep technical detail
- **Avoid:** Highly technical comparisons, expert-level scenarios

### Required Phrases:
- "Building on what you already know about [topic]"
- "You've probably encountered this in [common scenario]"
- "Taking this to the next level"
- "Here's how this connects to [previous concept]"
- "In practical terms for your team"
- "Let's add another layer"

### Content Parameters:
- **Sentence Length:** Maximum 20 words average
- **Paragraph Length:** Maximum 5 sentences
- **Technical Terms:** Maximum 10 per content piece
- **Examples:** Mix of workplace and industry-specific scenarios
- **Jargon:** Basic technical terms with brief explanations
- **Cognitive Load:** 5 main concepts maximum

### Example Patterns:
- Department-level business processes
- Common industry practices
- Standard tools and platforms in the field
- Team collaboration scenarios

---

## Level 3 - Advancing (Regular Engagement)

### Vocabulary Requirements:
- **Definition Standard:** Technical terms used without definition unless particularly complex
- **Complexity Limit:** Maximum 7 new concepts per content piece
- **Validation Points:** Strategic checkpoints every 500-600 words
- **Term Introduction:** Assume familiarity with standard industry terminology

### Analogies Framework:
- **Primary:** Cross-industry comparisons: "Like how financial services approach compliance"
- **Secondary:** Strategic frameworks: "Using Porter's Five Forces model"
- **Advanced:** Technical architecture patterns and methodologies
- **Expected:** Can handle abstract concepts and complex relationships

### Required Phrases:
- "Given your experience with [advanced topic]"
- "From a strategic perspective"
- "Considering the implementation complexities"
- "Advanced considerations include"
- "At the enterprise level"
- "For mature organizations"

### Content Parameters:
- **Sentence Length:** No restriction (maintain clarity)
- **Paragraph Length:** Maximum 7 sentences
- **Technical Terms:** Unlimited with context
- **Examples:** Industry case studies, benchmarks, best practices
- **Jargon:** Industry-standard terminology expected
- **Cognitive Load:** 7 main concepts maximum

### Example Patterns:
- Enterprise-wide implementations
- Industry benchmarks and standards
- Strategic initiatives and transformations
- Complex multi-stakeholder scenarios

## Progressive Complexity Matrix

| Element | Level 1 | Level 2 | Level 3 |
|---------|---------|---------|---------|
| **New Concepts** | 3 max | 5 max | 7 max |
| **Sentence Length** | 15 words avg | 20 words avg | Unrestricted |
| **Technical Terms** | 5 max (all defined) | 10 max (briefly defined) | Unlimited (context) |
| **Examples** | Everyday life | Workplace/Industry | Strategic/Enterprise |
| **Assumed Knowledge** | None | Basic familiarity | Regular engagement |
| **Validation Frequency** | Every 200 words | Every 300-400 words | Every 500-600 words |

---

## Level Transition Indicators

### Moving from Level 1 to Level 2:
- Audience can define basic terms without help
- Comfortable with fundamental concepts
- Asking "how" questions rather than "what" questions
- Ready for workplace applications

### Moving from Level 2 to Level 3:
- Audience can compare different approaches
- Understands trade-offs and implications
- Asking "why" and "which" questions
- Ready for strategic discussions

---

## Content Depth Guidelines

### Level 1 Depth:
- **Surface:** What it is and why it matters
- **Focus:** Basic understanding and relevance
- **Outcome:** Can explain to someone else

### Level 2 Depth:
- **Practical:** How it works and when to use it
- **Focus:** Application and implementation basics
- **Outcome:** Can apply in controlled scenarios

### Level 3 Depth:
- **Strategic:** Optimization, scaling, and innovation
- **Focus:** Advanced implementation and strategy
- **Outcome:** Can lead initiatives and make strategic decisions

---

## Failure Indicators by Level

### Level 1 Content Fails If:
- Uses undefined technical terms
- Assumes prior knowledge
- Examples too complex or industry-specific
- More than 3 main concepts introduced
- Lacks frequent validation points

### Level 2 Content Fails If:
- Jumps to advanced concepts without building
- No connection to previous knowledge
- Examples lack business context
- Missing practical applications
- Assumes expert-level understanding

### Level 3 Content Fails If:
- Over-explains basic concepts
- Lacks strategic perspective
- Examples too simplistic
- Missing industry context
- No advanced considerations
